🧘 What Is My Yoga Network?

My Yoga Network (MYN) is a cost-containment platform — not a yoga app — that connects people with real health conditions to clinically trained yoga therapists, safely, personally, and with medical credibility. Founded by Ram Bhakt, MYN operates as a 1-person holding company with 9 business divisions, powered by an AI-agent operating system (Strategy AGI) and a team of 16+ contributors across Riipen internships and an ASU capstone partnership.

The business model targets institutional buyers — hotels, hospitals, school districts, and corporate HR departments — not individual consumers. Revenue comes from B2B subscriptions ($800/month per patient in healthcare, $7K-$8.5K/month per hotel), with 72% gross margins enabled by a global network of C-IAYT certified yoga therapists. End users (patients, hotel guests, students) are adoption drivers, not the primary buyers.

MYN's competitive advantage is coordination — not content volume. We sell operational offload, risk transfer, consistency, and coverage. The competitive set is IAYT (authority but clunky), physical therapy networks, integrative medicine clinics, and forward-looking insurers. A new Wearables + EHR integration (ASU capstone) is building biometric proof that yoga therapy measurably improves HRV, sleep, and recovery — turning yoga from a subjective wellness activity into a data-driven therapeutic intervention.

💼 Business Model & How We Make Money

Who Pays Us (Institutional Buyers)
Hotels & Luxury Residential: $7K-$8.5K/month per property. Our anchor segment — highest willingness to pay, recurring, low price sensitivity. Build everything around this first.

Healthcare Systems: $800/month per patient, 72% margins. Strongest clinical evidence. They buy cost-containment, not wellness.

Corporate Wellness: Variable — HR budgets fluctuate. We only pursue standardized deployments. $1,200/employee yields $4,800 in savings.

Schools / Cities / Districts: Large budgets but slow procurement. Long nurture cycles. Compliance-ready case studies are the key.
What We Actually Sell
We sell operational offload, risk transfer, consistency, and coverage — not yoga sessions. The value proposition is:

For Hotels: "Turnkey on-site yoga that increases guest satisfaction, justifies premium pricing, and drives 5-star reviews."

For Healthcare: "Replace $24,000 in escalating medical interventions with $9,600 in therapeutic yoga — with biometric proof it works."

For Corporate: "Cut healthcare premiums and absenteeism with measurable results, not just engagement metrics."

End users (patients, guests, students) drive adoption but are not the buyers.

📣 Division Messaging & Positioning

MYN is NOT a yoga app. It is a cost-containment platform.

1. Healthcare (B2B2C)

Audience: Hospital administrators, healthcare CTOs, clinical directors, self-insured employers
"Replace $24,000 in escalating medical interventions with $9,600 in therapeutic yoga — with biometric proof it works."
Tone: Clinical, evidence-based, data-driven. Talking points: HRV improvement, condition-specific (chronic LBP, anxiety, cardiac), HSA/FSA fully allowable, 12-week pilot structure. Avoid: Wellness language, spiritual framing.

2. Corporate Wellness (B2B)

Audience: HR directors, benefits managers, wellness committee leads
"Cut healthcare premiums and absenteeism with a yoga program that delivers measurable results — not just engagement metrics."
Tone: Business-focused, ROI-oriented. Talking points: $1,200/employee → $4,800 savings, presenteeism reduction ($8K/employee/yr), on-site/virtual/hybrid. Avoid: Generic wellness platitudes.

3. Hotels + Hospitality (B2B)

Audience: Hotel GMs, hospitality group CTOs, revenue managers
"On-site yoga programs increase guest satisfaction, justify premium pricing, and drive 5-star reviews."
Tone: Partnership-oriented, revenue-focused. Talking points: RevPAR uplift, guest NPS improvement, turnkey program. Avoid: Healthcare/clinical language, cost-savings framing.

4. Yoga Therapy Marketplace

Audience: Patients seeking therapy, yoga therapists, instructors
"Find a certified yoga therapist who can treat your specific condition — backed by clinical evidence and biometric tracking."
Tone: Warm, accessible, professional. Talking points: C-IAYT certified directory, condition-specific matching, telehealth option. Avoid: Hard-sell language, insurance jargon.

5. Kids / Procurement

Audience: School administrators, after-school directors, parents
"Evidence-based yoga programs that improve focus, reduce anxiety, and build resilience in children."
Tone: Nurturing, safe, child-appropriate. Programs: After-school yoga, Mommy and Me classes, summer camps.

⌚ ASU Wearables + EHR Platform

By integrating biometric data from consumer wearables, My Yoga Network is building the first yoga organization to scientifically validate and quantify yoga's benefits. This project turns yoga from a subjective wellness activity into a data-driven therapeutic intervention — strengthening our reputation with hotels, corporate wellness partners, insurers, and direct consumers.

The platform tracks HRV, sleep stages, recovery scores, and activity metrics before/after yoga sessions. Target outcomes: HRV +10-20%, Deep sleep +15-30 min, Stress -15-25%, Recovery +10%. Product concept: "Yoga Bio Sync" — a premium, data-driven yoga program using biometric insights to personalize and validate yoga's impact.

👨‍💻 ASU Capstone Team — What Each Person Is Building

Bi-weekly sprints, 200 hours per student (1,000 total). Managed via Jira (YOGA board). Meetings every other week.

Anay Bhushan Pradhan — Project Lead / Auth & Integrations

apradh25@asu.edu | Component: Auth, Provider Integrations
What he's building: The OAuth abstraction layer — a plug-in architecture where each wearable provider (Oura, Apple Health, Fitbit, Garmin) shares the same interface for authentication, token refresh, and rate limiting. This is the foundation that makes adding new wearable providers a configuration change instead of a code rewrite.

Sprint 1: Oura Ring as first provider — complete OAuth flow with login, token refresh, 429 backoff handling, and secrets stored in vault.
Sprint 2: Extend to Apple Health and Fitbit via aggregator (Human API or Nabla) to avoid bespoke integrations for each.

David Brown — Infrastructure & Security

dbrow188@asu.edu | Component: Infrastructure, Security
What he's building: The secure foundation — DigitalOcean hosting environment, Auth0 tenant configured for HIPAA-safe authentication, and a vaulted token storage system for provider API keys. Also building the rules engine that translates raw metrics into actionable health flags.

Sprint 1: Stand up DigitalOcean environment, configure Auth0 tenant, build provider tokens vault table with rotation jobs and encryption at rest.
Sprint 2: Rules engine v1 — flags for low_sleep_night and high_stress_day that adapt session intensity and trigger nudges like "low recovery, pick a restorative session."

Benjamin Herman — EHR / FHIR Integration

bcherma1@asu.edu | Component: EHR Integrations, Security
What he's building: The medical records bridge — connecting our wearable data to Electronic Health Record systems via FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources). Starting with OpenEMR as the development target, with a path to Epic integration later. This is what makes MYN credible to hospitals and insurers.

Sprint 1: OpenEMR dev instance + FHIR Observation resource export spike. Export endpoint for biometric summaries with PHI minimization.
Sprint 2: HIPAA controls documentation + logging of all PHI access events. Building the compliance paper trail.

Roen Wainscoat — Data Pipelines & Analytics

rhwainsc@asu.edu | Component: Data Platform
What he's building: The data backbone — an ingest pipeline that pulls raw wearable data, deduplicates it, and computes daily health summaries. This powers everything downstream: therapist dashboards, patient progress reports, cohort research, and the metrics that prove yoga therapy works.

Sprint 1: provider_events table with dedupe keys and replay-safe processing. Daily metrics summary job computing sleep score, TST, HRV median, resting HR, readiness, activity minutes → stored as daily_metric_summaries.
Sprint 2: Cohort analytics draft + anonymization pipeline for research whitepapers and IRB-ready data.

Wyatt Belscher — UI/UX & Front-End

wbelsche@asu.edu | Component: Web App
What he's building: The therapist and patient experience — a "pre-session snapshot" card showing 7-day biometric trends and today's vitals inline for the therapist, with one-click copy to session notes. Also designing the patient-facing dashboards for goal tracking and progress visualization.

Sprint 1: Pre-session snapshot card (read-only) rendering last 7-day trends with copy-to-notes button. Figma designs for dashboards and goal-setting UI.
Sprint 2: Real-time HR overlay prototype for tele-sessions — optional live heart rate stream during virtual yoga sessions with safety thresholds and alerts.

Priya Patel — BioMedical Research (Supporting Role)

Riipen Intern | Research feeds into Wearables platform
What she's providing: The scientific foundation that validates everything the ASU team builds. Her 3-phase research program (literature review → wearable audit → pilot study) delivers the evidence base, the wearable recommendation (Oura Ring), and the pilot protocol (10 hotel guests, 4 weeks, 3 yoga classes/week).

Key outputs: 30-50 peer-reviewed study summaries, effect size data (HRV +10-25%), "Yoga Benefits Sheet" for sales, wearable comparison matrix, physician referral segment profiles, condition wedge analysis (chronic LBP vs. cancer recovery).

Phase II — Technical Audit (3 weeks)

Wearable accuracy comparison across HRV, sleep staging, low-movement reliability, API access, user friction. Result: Oura Ring recommended for pilot.

Phase III — Pilot Study (4 weeks)

10 hotel guests, Oura Ring, 4 weeks. 3 yoga classes/week + 1 Yoga Nidra. Measures: daily HRV, sleep stages, recovery, strain, PSS-10 questionnaires, attendance. Success = HRV +10-20%, deep sleep +15-30 min, stress -15-25%.
