🎬 Content System

Scott's content machine framework — the backbone of everything.

The Content Engine

  • Volume: Aim for ~100 pieces of content per day (memes, tweets, reels, clips)
  • Pipeline: Opus → Chop → Distribute (1-2 long-form weekly → 30-40 short clips)
  • Style: Gritty, not pretty. iPhone clips with close captions. Authenticity over polish.
  • Budget: 50-60% of all effort/spend should go to content creation
  • Reinvestment: Spend new income on producing more content
  • Automation: AI auto-caption, generate clips, repurpose transcripts into blogs + emails
📱

Content Types

Memes, tweets, short reels, iPhone clips with captions, long-form video (opus), blog posts from transcripts, email sequences.

🌐 Scott's Take on myn.com

What Scott Hoffman would say about the public marketing website — applying his frameworks to every page.

What Scott Would Love

  • Vertical pages exist: Hotels, Healthcare, Corporate Wellness, After-School — each has its own page. Scott's original advice was "niche classes down" and you've done that at the page level.
  • Therapist directory: /find-a-yoga-therapist-near-me is exactly the "tribe building" play — people searching by condition = warm leads who self-identify their problem.
  • Instructor onboarding page: /join-as-yoga-instructor maps to his 2018 idea of "Want to become an instructor?" as a consistent footer/CTA across the site.
  • Blog system: He pushed hard on content being evergreen — "content stays online and stays there forever, vs ads." The blog is your SEO asset layer.

What Scott Would Push You On

  • "Where's the video?" His #1 principle was video-first content — YouTube on Monday, blog Thursday, sponsored FB Saturday. myn.com needs embedded video authority clips on every vertical page.
